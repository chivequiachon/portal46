About Me


Chive (チャイブ)

I am a Software Engineer and a musician who simply loves Japanese music, anime, and Sakamichi Series!


# Where it all started

I started out as a fan of AKB48 (until now although I haven't followed them since then) when I accidentally watched Shin Domoto Kyoudai around late 2012, where Takamina was one of the MCs. The concept of the show eventually got interesting for me and also sparked my curiosity for Takamina along with AKB48. From there, I started watching Akbingo, especially Shoujiki Shougi, then proceeded by watching their MVs and listening to their music. The first music and MV that I liked from them was not their popular ones like HebiRote or Aitakatta, it was Gingham Check.

Yuko!!!!!!

> So, How did I discover Nogizaka46?

It was a mere coincidence. There were actually two instances that I stumbled upon them. The first one was when I watched Magi: The Labyrinth of Magic. Yes! you already have an idea by now. It was Magi's first ending theme, "Yubi Bōenkyō".

The second instance was the one that got me to them. Around late 2013, vid48 was still existent. It was a website that features music videos and subbed shows from different idol groups in Japan although most videos are from Akb48 (hence the name). One time, while I was browsing the website, I saw a thumbnail of Nogizaka46's Barrette. It somehow caught my attention because it seemed so dark and I thought that it was a bit unusual compared to the idol image I had in my mind. I clicked and watched it and I was not wrong, it was really dark.

Imagine seeing these scenes for the first time. Interesting isn't it?


Miona's image back then really fits in with this song's mood. Maybe because of the pressure she felt when she suddenly got selected as the center for this single along with the fact that she's still new to the group.

It was a very new concept and it was interesting at least but not up to the point that I liked it. That was also the time when they released "Tsuki No Ookisa" as one of Naruto Shippuuden's opening theme. I watched the MV and again, it caught my attention.

Ninjas and sword fights!


Their concept eventually grew on me as time goes by. Back to the same pattern again. I then watched NogiDoko and it was really fun. I can still remember the first episode I watched. It was during the waterfall hit challenge wherein they gave prayers at the bottom of the waterfall. That was also the first time where I saw Nanamin laughing at Ikuchan's unfortunate accident LOL!

And the rest is history.....


# So, Why do I like them?

Here are the major factors for me:
1) Sound
2) Music Videos
3) Costumes/fashion
4) Transparency

Let's just say it's because of the difference. They are beautiful, they have long skirts, they portray elegance (their dances, their costumes, and the way they act), and they are compact as a group.

Another thing is transparency. I admit, I don't know anything about Akb48 members' personal life as compared to Nogizaka (It's not even Akb's concept to begin with). NogiDoko and NogiChuu usually talks about member's skills, experiences, or about their personal life, most especially about their families. Just like how Ashu was getting fed by her mother every morning (she's living by herself now) or how Nanamin talks about how poor her family was before. It gives me a realistic view of how human they are.
